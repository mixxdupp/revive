ID: firstaid-burn-blister
NAME: Burn Blister Management
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-burns/basics/art-20056649
  STEP 1: Cool the burn under cool running water for 10-20 minutes.
  STEP 2: Do NOT pop burn blisters — the fluid protects healing skin.
  STEP 3: Cover loosely with non-stick gauze or clean cloth.
  STEP 4: Elevate the burned area above heart level if possible.
  STEP 5: Watch for infection: increasing redness, pus, fever, red streaks.

ID: firstaid-burn-char
NAME: Severe Burn (Third Degree)
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-burns/basics/art-20056649
  STEP 1: Call emergency services immediately. This is life-threatening.
  STEP 2: Do NOT remove burnt clothing stuck to the skin.
  STEP 3: Cover loosely with clean, dry cloth. Do NOT apply water.
  STEP 4: Elevate burned area above heart level.
  STEP 5: Treat for shock: lay flat, keep warm, monitor breathing.

ID: firstaid-head-trauma
NAME: Head Trauma Assessment
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-head-trauma/basics/art-20056626
  STEP 1: Keep the person still. Do NOT move them unless in immediate danger.
  STEP 2: Check consciousness: ask their name, where they are, what happened.
  STEP 3: Control any bleeding with gentle direct pressure.
  STEP 4: Check for clear fluid leaking from ears or nose.
  STEP 5: Monitor every 15 minutes: pupils, consciousness, breathing.

ID: firstaid-head-concussion
NAME: Concussion Management
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/concussion/symptoms-causes/syc-20355594
  STEP 1: Remove person from activity. Physical and mental rest is critical.
  STEP 2: Apply cold pack to the impact area for 20 minutes.
  STEP 3: Monitor for worsening symptoms over 24-48 hours.
  STEP 4: Wake the person every 2-3 hours for the first night.
  STEP 5: No blood-thinning painkillers (aspirin/ibuprofen) for 24 hours.

ID: firstaid-concussion
NAME: Concussion Quick Guide
SOURCE: https://www.cdc.gov/traumatic-brain-injury/index.html
  STEP 1: Stop all activity. Sit them down in a safe location.
  STEP 2: Check pupils: shine light in each eye. Both should be equal size.
  STEP 3: Test orientation: name, location, day of the week.
  STEP 4: Check balance: stand with eyes closed for 30 seconds.
  STEP 5: Monitor for 24 hours. Wake every 2 hours during sleep.

ID: firstaid-choking
NAME: Choking Response (Heimlich)
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-choking/basics/art-20056637
  STEP 1: Ask 'Are you choking?' — if they can't speak or cough, act immediately.
  STEP 2: Stand behind the person, wrap arms around their waist.
  STEP 3: Make a fist, place thumb side against abdomen above navel.
  STEP 4: Grasp fist with other hand. Thrust inward and upward sharply.
  STEP 5: If object doesn't dislodge: alternate 5 back blows with 5 thrusts.

ID: firstaid-cpr
NAME: CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
SOURCE: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/cpr/performing-cpr/cpr-steps
  STEP 1: Check responsiveness. Tap shoulders and shout.
  STEP 2: Place heel of hand on center of chest, between nipples.
  STEP 3: Compress chest 2 inches deep at 100-120 beats per minute.
  STEP 4: Give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions.
  STEP 5: Continue 30:2 cycle without stopping until help arrives.

ID: firstaid-heimlich
NAME: Heimlich Maneuver (Choking)
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-choking/basics/art-20056637
  STEP 1: Confirm choking — person cannot speak, cough, or breathe.
  STEP 2: Stand behind the person and wrap arms around their waist.
  STEP 3: Make a fist with one hand, place thumb side against abdomen above navel.
  STEP 4: Thrust inward and upward with quick, forceful movements.

ID: firstaid-shock
NAME: Treating Shock
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-shock/basics/art-20056620
  STEP 1: Recognize shock: pale/cool/clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion.
  STEP 2: Lay the person flat and elevate legs 8-12 inches.
  STEP 3: Maintain body temperature — cover with blankets or coats.
  STEP 4: Do NOT give food or water. Moisten lips only.

ID: firstaid-snakebite
NAME: Snakebite Management
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-snake-bites/basics/art-20056681
  STEP 1: Move away from the snake. Do NOT try to catch or kill it.
  STEP 2: Keep the bitten limb below heart level and immobilize it.
  STEP 3: Do NOT cut, suck, tourniquet, or apply ice to the bite.
  STEP 4: Mark the edge of swelling and note the time.
  STEP 5: Evacuate to a hospital. Antivenom is the only treatment.

ID: firstaid-anaphylaxis
NAME: Anaphylaxis Response
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-anaphylaxis/basics/art-20056608
  STEP 1: Recognize: throat tightening, facial swelling, hives, wheezing, dizziness.

ID: firstaid-hypothermia
NAME: Hypothermia Treatment
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/hypothermia/symptoms-causes/syc-20352682
  STEP 1: Get the person out of the cold and remove wet clothing.
  STEP 2: Insulate from the ground and cover with multiple layers.
  STEP 3: Apply warm (not hot) packs to core areas: neck, armpits, groin.
  STEP 4: Give warm sweet drinks if conscious and alert.
  STEP 5: Handle gently — a severely hypothermic heart is fragile.

ID: firstaid-heatstroke
NAME: Heat Stroke Treatment
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-heatstroke/basics/art-20056655
  STEP 1: Recognize: hot/red/dry skin, confusion, temp over 104°F, loss of consciousness.
  STEP 2: Move to shade. Remove excess clothing.
  STEP 3: Cool aggressively: wet the entire body and fan.
  STEP 4: Evacuate immediately. Call emergency services.

ID: firstaid-altitude
NAME: Altitude Sickness
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/altitude-sickness/symptoms-causes/syc-20369524
  STEP 1: Recognize: headache, nausea, dizziness, fatigue above 8,000 ft.
  STEP 2: Stop ascending. Rest at current altitude.
  STEP 3: If symptoms worsen or don't improve in 24 hours: descend 1,000+ feet.
  STEP 4: Emergency: give supplemental oxygen if available. Descend immediately.

ID: firstaid-choking-response
NAME: Choking Response (Adult & Child)
SOURCE: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-choking/basics/art-20056637
  STEP 1: Determine severity: can they cough, speak, or breathe?

  STEP 4: Immobilize the bitten limb with a splint. Movement of muscles pumps lymph fluid, spreading venom.
  STEP 5: For hemotoxic snakes (vipers, rattlesnakes): do NOT apply compression bandage. Keep limb below heart level and evacuate.
