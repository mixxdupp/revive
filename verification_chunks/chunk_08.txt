ID: tools-bowline
NAME: Bowline Knot
SOURCE: https://www.animatedknots.com/
  STEP 1: Form a small loop in the standing part of the rope.
  STEP 2: Pass the working end UP through the loop.
  STEP 3: Go around behind the standing part.
  STEP 4: Pass back DOWN through the same loop.

ID: tools-clove-hitch
NAME: Clove Hitch
SOURCE: https://www.animatedknots.com/
  STEP 1: Wrap the rope around the post once.
  STEP 2: Cross over the first wrap and wrap around the post again.
  STEP 3: Tuck the working end under the second wrap.

ID: tools-taut-line
NAME: Taut-Line Hitch
SOURCE: https://www.animatedknots.com/
  STEP 1: Loop rope around anchor (tree, stake).
  STEP 2: Make 2 wraps inside the loop around the standing part.
  STEP 3: Make 1 wrap outside the loop above the coils.
  STEP 4: Slide the knot to adjust tension.

ID: tools-square-lashing
NAME: Square Lashing
SOURCE: https://www.animatedknots.com/
  STEP 1: Start with a clove hitch on the vertical pole.
  STEP 2: Weave over and under alternately around both poles.
  STEP 3: Make 3-4 complete wrapping turns.
  STEP 4: Frapping: wrap tightly between the poles to tighten.
  STEP 5: Finish with a clove hitch on the horizontal pole.

  STEP 1: Gather long, flexible materials: willow shoots, cattail leaves, grass bundles, split roots, or bark strips.
  STEP 2: Start with a simple over-under pattern: lay warp fibers parallel, weave weft fibers perpendicular.

ID: tools-natural-adhesive
NAME: Natural Adhesive (Pine Pitch Glue)
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Collect pine resin — scrape from wounds on coniferous trees. You need about a golf ball-sized lump.
  STEP 2: Melt the resin in a small rock depression or bark container near heat (not over flames — it's flammable).
  STEP 3: Mix in finely powdered charcoal (1:3 charcoal to resin ratio) while the resin is liquid.
  STEP 4: Add a small amount of finely shredded plant fiber or animal hair for additional reinforcement.
  STEP 5: Apply while warm and liquid. It sets as it cools into a strong, waterproof bond.

ID: tools-emergency-rope
NAME: Emergency Rope Making
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Harvest long, strong plant fibers: inner bark of basswood/linden, yucca leaves, nettle stalks, cattail leaves.
  STEP 2: Process fibers: soak in water to soften, then split into thin, consistent strands.
  STEP 3: Reverse-wrap technique: take 2 fiber bundles. Twist each clockwise, then wrap them around each other counter-clockwise.
  STEP 4: Splice new fibers in: when one bundle runs short, lay a new fiber alongside it overlapping 3+ inches, and continue twisting.
  STEP 5: Test strength progressively. Two-ply cordage from basswood bark can support 100+ pounds.

ID: tools-fire-hardening
NAME: Fire Hardening Wood
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Carve your tool to the desired shape while the wood is still green and workable.
  STEP 2: Hold the carved tip over low, steady coals.
  STEP 3: The wood should turn a deep tan to light brown color.
