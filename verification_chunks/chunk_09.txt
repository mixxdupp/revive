ID: water-uv-sun-purification
NAME: UV Sun Purification (SODIS)
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Fill a clear plastic or glass bottle with water. Pre-filter if turbid — water must be clear.
  STEP 2: Lay the bottle on a reflective surface (metal sheet, foil, light-colored rock) in full sun.
  STEP 3: Leave in direct sunlight for minimum 6 hours. If partly cloudy, extend to 2 full days.
  STEP 4: Shake the bottle every hour to oxygenate — the combination of UV + oxygen is more lethal to pathogens.
  STEP 5: Water is safe to drink after the treatment period. This method kills bacteria, viruses, and protozoa.

ID: water-rationing-schedule
NAME: Water Rationing Schedule
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: NEVER ration water below 500mL (1 pint) per day — your body needs minimum water to function.
  STEP 2: Priority 1: sip small amounts frequently rather than gulping large amounts at once.
  STEP 3: Priority 2: minimize water loss — rest in shade during heat, breathe through your nose, avoid sweating.
  STEP 4: Priority 3: eat less if water is scarce — digestion requires water.
  STEP 5: Signs of dehydration: dark urine, headache, dizziness, rapid heartbeat. If these appear, increase intake immediately.

ID: water-water-testing-field
NAME: Field Water Testing
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Visual test: clear water is NOT necessarily safe, but cloudy, colored, or oily water is definitely suspect.
  STEP 2: Smell test: if water smells of chemicals, sulfur (rotten eggs), or sewage — do not drink. Odorless is better but not proof of safety.
  STEP 3: Flow test: flowing water is generally safer than stagnant water. Spring sources are safest natural water.
  STEP 4: Animal indicators: animals drinking from a source is a PARTIALLY positive sign, but animals tolerate pathogens humans cannot.
  STEP 5: When in doubt: ALWAYS purify. Boiling (1 minute rolling boil) kills all biological pathogens.

ID: water-ice-melting-technique
NAME: Ice Melting for Water
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: NEVER eat snow directly — it lowers core temperature and causes dehydration (your body uses more water to melt it).

ID: water-double-boil-purification
NAME: Double-Boil Purification
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Set up: place dirty water in a pot over fire. Cover with an angled lid (metal, flat rock, or bark).
  STEP 2: Place a clean collection container at the low edge of the lid to catch condensate drips.

  STEP 4: Bring the dirty water to a steady simmer — not a rolling boil. Excess heat wastes fuel and reduces condensation.
  STEP 5: This produces DISTILLED water — safe from biological AND chemical contaminants.
