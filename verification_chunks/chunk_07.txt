ID: shelter-wind-assessment
NAME: Wind Direction Assessment
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Wet your finger and hold it up — the cool side faces the wind.
  STEP 2: Drop light debris (grass, dust, fine snow) and watch which direction it drifts.
  STEP 3: Look at tree lean and flag trees — they indicate PREVAILING (long-term) wind direction.
  STEP 4: Check snow drift patterns, sand dunes, and cloud movement for wind direction.
  STEP 5: Orient your shelter entrance 90° to the prevailing wind. Place the shelter's back wall into the wind.

ID: shelter-site-drainage-check
NAME: Shelter Site Drainage Check
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Look for water stain marks on trees and rocks — they show previous flood levels.
  STEP 2: Check soil moisture: dig 2 inches down. If wet, water table is too high.
  STEP 3: Pour water on the ground and watch where it flows. Your shelter should be UPHILL of the flow path.
  STEP 4: Avoid the bottom of valleys, gullies, and ravines — these collect cold air and water.
  STEP 5: Ideal site: mid-slope, slightly elevated, with natural drainage away from the shelter footprint.

ID: shelter-thermal-trap
NAME: Thermal Trap Shelter
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9780062378071
  STEP 1: Build the smallest shelter that fits you — smaller interior = faster warming from body heat alone.
  STEP 2: Use a sunken entrance (dig entry below floor level) to create a cold air well.
