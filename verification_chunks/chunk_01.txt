ID: env-flash-flood-adv
NAME: Flash Flood Response
SOURCE: https://www.ready.gov/floods
  STEP 1: Move to high ground IMMEDIATELY. Do not wait to gather belongings.
  STEP 2: Never cross flowing water deeper than your ankles.
  STEP 3: If swept away: float on your back with feet downstream.
  STEP 4: If in a vehicle: abandon it if water starts rising around it.

ID: env-post-flood
NAME: Post-Flood Survival
SOURCE: https://www.ready.gov/floods
  STEP 1: Do NOT drink floodwater or any water contaminated by flooding.
  STEP 2: Avoid walking through standing floodwater.
  STEP 3: Discard all food that contacted floodwater.
  STEP 4: Watch for structural collapse in buildings.

ID: env-ocean-adrift
NAME: Ocean Survival (Adrift)
SOURCE: https://www.uscgboating.org
  STEP 1: Conserve energy above all else. Float, don't swim.
  STEP 2: Protect yourself from sun and cold water.
  STEP 3: Collect rainwater immediately when it falls.
  STEP 4: Fish near floating debris — it attracts marine life.
  STEP 5: Signal constantly — use anything reflective or bright.

ID: env-coastal-tidal-adv
NAME: Coastal & Tidal Hazards
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Rip current: SWIM PARALLEL to shore, never against the current.
  STEP 2: Tidal cut-offs: check tide tables before exploring coastal areas.
  STEP 3: Storm surge: evacuate to high ground before the storm arrives.
  STEP 4: Tsunami warning signs: rapidly receding water, unusual roar from sea.

ID: env-tropical-disease-adv
NAME: Tropical Disease Prevention
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Prevent mosquito bites: cover all skin, especially at dawn and dusk.
  STEP 2: Sleep under a net or in a sealed shelter.
  STEP 3: Purify ALL water in tropical environments.
  STEP 4: Treat any cut or scratch immediately — tropical infections spread fast.
  STEP 5: Monitor for fever, chills, severe headache — signs of tropical infection.

ID: env-desert-night-adv
NAME: Desert Night Travel
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Travel only at night in extreme desert heat.
  STEP 2: Navigate by stars: Polaris (north) or Southern Cross (south).
  STEP 3: Watch for nocturnal wildlife — scorpions, snakes are active at night.
  STEP 4: Stay warm — deserts drop 40-50°F at night.

ID: env-desert-shelter-adv
NAME: Desert Shelter
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Create shade — even 15°F cooler in shade vs. full sun.
  STEP 2: Dig a trench 18 inches deep and lie in it.
  STEP 3: Elevate yourself off the ground surface if possible.

ID: env-desert-water-adv
NAME: Finding Water in the Desert
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Look for green vegetation — it always indicates water nearby.
  STEP 2: Follow animal tracks downhill — they lead to water.
  STEP 3: Dig in the outside bend of dry riverbeds.
  STEP 4: Make a solar still: plastic sheet over a hole with a collection container.

ID: env-jungle-movement-adv
NAME: Jungle Movement
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Follow waterways downstream — they lead to civilization.
  STEP 2: Use a stick to probe ahead and clear spider webs.
  STEP 3: Step on logs, not over them. Check before grabbing vines.
  STEP 4: Move slowly during the hottest hours. Pace yourself.

ID: env-jungle-travel-plan
NAME: Jungle Travel Planning
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Travel in the early morning (6-10 AM) and late afternoon (3-6 PM).
  STEP 2: Use ridgelines when possible — they're above the undergrowth.
  STEP 3: Mark your trail to prevent circling.
  STEP 4: Camp early — set up shelter before dark (5 PM in tropics).

ID: env-jungle-shelter-adv
NAME: Jungle Shelter
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9780062378071
  STEP 1: Build elevated — a platform or hammock at least 2 feet off the ground.
  STEP 2: Roof with large overlapping leaves — banana, palm, or similar.
  STEP 3: Clear vegetation 3 feet around your shelter — airflow deters mosquitoes.
  STEP 4: Dig drainage channels around your shelter footprint.

ID: env-jungle-water-adv
NAME: Jungle Water Sources
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9780062378071
  STEP 1: Cut green bamboo — it contains drinkable water in the stems.
  STEP 2: Collect water from large-leaf plants early morning.
  STEP 3: Use vine water: cut vine at the TOP first, then cut bottom to drain.
  STEP 4: Running streams are safest — still water is highest risk.

ID: env-arctic-shelter-adv
NAME: Arctic Shelter Building
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Use snow as your primary building material — it's an excellent insulator.
  STEP 2: Build a quinzhee: pile snow 5 feet high, let it settle 2 hours, hollow it out.
  STEP 3: Always create a ventilation hole in the roof — CO2 can kill you.
  STEP 4: Build a sleeping platform ABOVE the entrance — warm air rises.
  STEP 5: Block the entrance with a pack or snow block at night.

ID: firstaid-superglue-suture
NAME: Superglue Wound Closure
SOURCE: https://www.healthline.com/health/super-glue-on-cuts
  STEP 1: Clean and dry the wound thoroughly. Stop bleeding first.
  STEP 2: Push wound edges together precisely.
  STEP 3: Apply thin layer of glue ON TOP of the crack, not inside.
  STEP 4: Hold shut for 60 seconds until dry.

ID: firstaid-finger-amputation
NAME: Trapped Digit Amputation
SOURCE: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK13317/
  STEP 1: Verify impossibility of rescue. Tourniquet base of finger.
  STEP 2: Cut skin and tendons around the bone (circumferential).
  STEP 3: Locate the joint (knuckle). Bend finger to open joint gap.
  STEP 4: Complete separation. Apply pressure dressing.

ID: tools-prusik-knot
NAME: Prusik Knot
SOURCE: https://www.animatedknots.com/
  STEP 1: Take a smaller loop of cord (prussik loop) behind the main rope.
  STEP 2: Pass the knot end through the loop center 3 times, wrapping the main rope.
  STEP 3: Pull the knot tight and dress the coils.
  STEP 4: Test grip: Pull on loop to lock, push barrel to slide.

  STEP 1: Travel only at night or during dawn/dusk.

NAME: Desert Shade Shelter
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Create a double-layer shade with 12-inch air gap.
  STEP 2: Raise the shelter 18 inches off the ground.
  STEP 3: Orient the open side away from prevailing wind.
  STEP 4: If no materials: dig a trench and cover with any available material.

ID: env-desert-water
NAME: Finding Water in Desert
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Look for green vegetation — especially cottonwood, willow, and palm.
  STEP 2: Dig in the outside bend of dry washes and riverbeds.
  STEP 3: Watch for birds (especially doves) at dusk — they fly toward water.
  STEP 4: Check rock crevices and shaded north-facing cliff bases.

ID: env-jungle-travel
NAME: Jungle Travel
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Follow streams downstream — they lead to rivers, rivers lead to people.
  STEP 2: Travel on ridgelines when possible — less vegetation, better visibility.
  STEP 3: Mark your path — the jungle looks identical in every direction.
  STEP 4: Watch your feet and where you grab — snakes and insects are everywhere.

ID: env-jungle-shelter
NAME: Jungle Hammock Shelter
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9780062378071
  STEP 1: Sleep OFF the ground. Always.
  STEP 2: Use poncho or tarp as a rain fly above your sleeping area.
  STEP 3: Tuck mosquito netting under your sleeping surface.
  STEP 4: Build a smudge fire nearby to drive away insects.

ID: env-arctic-shelter
NAME: Snow Shelter (Quinzhee)
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Pile snow into a mound 7-8 feet tall, 10 feet wide.
  STEP 2: Let the mound settle for 1-2 hours (sintering).
  STEP 3: Insert sticks 12 inches deep all over the mound.
  STEP 4: Hollow from a low entrance upward. Sleep platform above entrance.
  STEP 5: Poke a fist-sized ventilation hole in the roof.

ID: env-arctic-travel
NAME: Arctic Travel & Navigation
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Travel during warmest hours. Shelter during extreme cold.
  STEP 2: Manage layers to prevent sweating.
  STEP 3: Use snowshoes or improvised floatation to prevent post-holing.
  STEP 4: Watch for thin ice: dark ice, snow-covered ice, and ice near moving water.

ID: env-arctic-frostbite
NAME: Frostbite Prevention & Treatment
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Recognize: white/gray waxy skin, numbness, hardness.
  STEP 2: Rewarm in body heat: armpit, groin, stomach.
  STEP 3: If possible, rewarm in 98-102°F water for 20-30 minutes.
  STEP 4: Do NOT rewarm if refreezing is possible.

ID: env-mountain-lightning
NAME: Lightning Safety in Mountains
SOURCE: https://www.weather.gov/safety/lightning
  STEP 1: Get below treeline and away from peaks, ridges, and isolated trees.
  STEP 2: Assume the lightning crouch: squat on balls of feet, arms around knees.
  STEP 3: Stay away from water, metal, and wet rock.
  STEP 4: Groups should spread 50+ feet apart.

ID: env-mountain-altitude
NAME: Mountain Altitude Management
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Above 8,000 ft: ascend no more than 1,000 ft per day.
  STEP 2: Climb high, sleep low.
  STEP 3: Hydrate aggressively — 3-4 liters per day at altitude.
  STEP 4: Golden rule: if symptoms worsen, descend immediately.

  STEP 1: If caught: swim aggressively to stay on surface.

ID: env-quicksand
NAME: Quicksand Escape
SOURCE: https://openlibrary.org/isbn/9781440579776
  STEP 1: Stop moving. Do NOT struggle or kick.
  STEP 2: Drop pack and heavy items.
  STEP 3: Lean back slowly. Float on your back.
  STEP 4: Wiggle legs to break suction. Pull out one at a time.

ID: env-tsunami-response
NAME: Tsunami Response
SOURCE: https://www.tsunami.gov/
  STEP 1: If you feel a strong earthquake near the coast, move to high ground immediately.
  STEP 2: Watch for natural warning signs: ocean suddenly pulling far from shore.
  STEP 3: Move inland or to elevation above 100 feet. Go UP, not just away.
  STEP 4: Stay at high ground for hours. Tsunamis come in multiple waves.

ID: env-heat-exhaustion

NAME: Rib Fracture Management
SOURCE: https://www.redcross.org.uk/first-aid/learn-first-aid/broken-bone
  STEP 1: Confirm rib fracture: point tenderness on ribs, pain worse with breathing/coughing.
  STEP 2: DO NOT wrap or bind the chest tightly.
  STEP 3: Practice deep breathing exercises every hour despite the pain.
  STEP 4: Position for sleep: sleep on the INJURED side.
  STEP 5: Monitor for pneumothorax: sudden worsening, one-sided chest expansion.

ID: firstaid-choking-complete
NAME: Choking Response (Complete Guide)
SOURCE: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/first-aid
  STEP 1: Assess: can they cough, speak, or breathe? If yes — encourage coughing.
  STEP 2: Give 5 sharp back blows between the shoulder blades with heel of your hand.
  STEP 3: If back blows fail: 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  STEP 4: Alternate: 5 back blows, then 5 abdominal thrusts until cleared.
  STEP 5: For infants: 5 back blows with baby face-down on your forearm, then 5 chest thrusts.

ID: firstaid-eye-field
NAME: Eye Injury (Field Treatment)
SOURCE: https://archive.org/search?query=FM+21-76+Survival+Manual
  STEP 1: Foreign object: flush eye with clean water for 15+ minutes.
  STEP 2: Impaled object: DO NOT REMOVE IT. Stabilize in place.
  STEP 3: Chemical exposure: flush continuously for 20+ minutes.
  STEP 4: Snow blindness: patch both eyes with dark cloth for 24 hours.
